# TP53 Bioinformatics Analysis Pipeline

This repository contains a beginner bioinformatics workflow project focused on the analysis of the human tumor suppressor protein TP53 using computational biology tools and databases.

The project explores sequence analysis, homologous protein identification, multiple sequence alignment, and protein structure visualization to understand evolutionary conservation and structural organization of TP53 across species.

---

# Project Overview

TP53 is one of the most important tumor suppressor proteins involved in:

* DNA repair
* Cell cycle regulation
* Apoptosis
* Genome stability

Due to its critical biological role, TP53 is commonly referred to as the “Guardian of the Genome.”

This project investigates TP53 from multiple bioinformatics perspectives including:

* sequence analysis
* evolutionary comparison
* structural biology
* protein conservation

# Completed Analyses

## 1. Protein Sequence Analysis (BLASTP)

Human TP53 protein sequence was retrieved from UniProt and analyzed using NCBI BLASTP to identify homologous protein sequences across species.

### Objectives

* Identify homologous proteins
* Study sequence similarity
* Analyze evolutionary conservation

### Tools Used

* UniProt
* NCBI BLASTP

### Key Concepts

* FASTA format
* Accession numbers
* Sequence homology
* Evolutionary conservation

---

## 2. Multiple Sequence Alignment (MSA)

TP53 protein sequences from multiple organisms were aligned using Clustal Omega.

### Species Compared

* Human
* Gorilla
* Mouse
* Dog
* Zebrafish

### Objectives

* Identify conserved regions
* Observe mutations and gaps
* Study sequence variation across evolution

### Tools Used

* Clustal Omega

---

## 3. Protein Structure Prediction & Visualization

The 3D structure of TP53 protein was visualized and analyzed to understand protein folding and structural organization.

### Structural Features Observed

* Alpha helices
* Beta sheets
* Loop/coil regions

### Biological Importance

Protein structure directly influences protein function. Structural changes in TP53 can affect DNA binding and tumor suppressor activity.
